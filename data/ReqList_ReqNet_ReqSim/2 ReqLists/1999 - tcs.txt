3.0.1	The TCS system will be capable of controlling the Predator and Outrider AVs with a single control system, using existing military services standard hardware and software, and supporting interfaces with various C4I systems.
3.0.2	The TCS shall consist of the TCS workstation Hardware Configuration Items (HWCIs), TCS Computer Software Configuration Items (CSCIs), and additional TCS Support HWCIs and CSCIs.
3.0.3	All TCS HWCI’s and CSCI’s shall be Year 2000 compliant.
3.0.4	The primary function of the TCS is to provide command and control of the payload, Air Vehicle (AV), data link, and other necessary support equipment in order to employ tactical UAVs to conduct reconnaissance, surveillance, target acquisition, and target identification missions.  The TCS will interface with and export and disseminate payload data to military supported units via external (not part of TCS) tactical communications systems, and C4I systems.  Communications procedures, formats, and interfaces will be interoperable with selected standard DoD C4I systems, architectures, and protocols.
3.0.5	The operational capabilities to be performed by the system will be determined by task analysis in accordance with MIL STD 1388 Task 401 as a guide based on a thorough understanding of Outrider and Predator mission requirements. Tasks will be evaluated and allocated based on operator skills and proficiencies. The initial TCS task analysis will produce a system baseline which will be optimized by engineering analysis and operator evaluations.
3.0.6	The TCS shall meet the applicable capability and characteristic criteria established by the Operational Requirements Document (ORD) CAF 003-90-I-A for the RQ-1A Predator Medium Altitude Endurance Unmanned Aerial Vehicle/System (MAE UAV) and the Close Range – Tactical Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (CR-TUAV). Appendix B and C identify applicable capability and characteristic criteria to TCS.
3.0.7	The TCS shall support 5 levels of UAV interaction: Level 1: receipt and transmission of secondary imagery and/or [as well as] data; Level 2: direct receipt of imagery and/or [as well as] data; Level 3: control of the UAV payload in addition to direct receipt of imagery/data; Level 4: control of the UAV, less launch and recovery, plus all the functions of level three; Level 5: capability to have full function and control of the UAV from takeoff to landing
3.0.8	The TCS system shall provide software capabilities and hardware configurations necessary to fulfill the operational tasking requirements across the 5 levels of interaction.
3.1.0.1	The states of operation of the TCS shall include Startup, Operation, and Shutdown.
3.1.0.2	The TCS states shall not exist concurrently.
3.1.1.0.1	Upon application of power the TCS shall enter the Startup State.
3.1.1.0.2	The Startup State shall be comprised of the following modes: Normal Startup Mode and Recovery Startup Mode.
3.1.1.0.3	The TCS will execute the particular startup mode which corresponds with the manner in which the TCS Software was halted.
3.1.1.0.4	When the TCS Software is terminated normally the TCS shall enter the Normal Startup Mode of operation upon application of power.
3.1.1.0.5	When the TCS software is halted due to an unplanned power interruption as well as abnormal program termination, then the TCS shall enter the Recovery Startup Mode upon application of power.
3.1.1.0.6	During startup, the TCS shall determine which of the 5 levels of interaction are achievable by the TCS configuration.
3.1.1.0.7	Levels of interaction higher than those achievable by a particular TCS configuration shall be prohibited.
3.1.1.0.8	The TCS shall inform the operator if the operator attempts to execute a function prohibited as a result of the determined level of interaction.
3.1.1.1.0.1	When executing in the Normal Startup Mode, the TCS shall provide the system functionality necessary to initialize the system to place it in the Operations State within 60 seconds from the time power is supplied and the TCS application is launched.
3.1.1.1.0.2	Initialization of the TCS HWCIs shall include startup of HWCIs, download of software, startup of CSCIs, execution of Startup FD/L, and establishment of the state of readiness of all interfaces.
3.1.1.2.0.1	The TCS in the Recovery Startup Mode shall provide the system functionality to resume the Operations State within 45 seconds.
3.1.1.2.0.2	Recovery of the TCS HWCIs shall include startup of HWCIs, download of software, startup of CSCIs, and establishment of the state of readiness of all interfaces.
3.1.1.2.0.3	The TCS shall be capable of automatically recording system state data, interface communications and other information necessary to support event reconstruction.
3.1.1.2.0.4	For recovery from abnormal termination , TCS shall prompt the operator to select the type of recovery to be executed: (1) Resume in the Same Modes And Data; (2) Resume in the Same Modes but Review and Modify the Command Data as Necessary; or, (3) Perform a Command Shutdown and Startup Via the Normal Startup Mode.

3.1.2.0.2	The Operations State modes shall not exist concurrently.
3.1.2.0.3	The operator shall have the capability to command the system to the Shutdown State from all modes under the Operations State.
3.1.2.0.4	The TCS hardware and software shall execute periodic Fault Detection/Location (FD/L) while in the Normal Operations Mode and Training Mode, to include a periodic determination of level of interaction.

Perform Software Upgrades; 7. Perform Software Debug and Monitoring

3.1.3.0.3	There shall be no modes of operation in the shutdown state.

3.11.3.1	The Design features shall be included to: 1. minimize the number and frequency of required preventive maintenance actions based on performance requirements and lowest life cycle costs; 2. minimize the contribution to degradation of TCS equipment reliability as a consequence of performing either preventive as well as corrective maintenance; 3. enable the performance of all maintenance actions with safety and comparative ease by providing adequate access to all equipment components and minimizing the requirements for special tools and test equipment; 4. minimize the requirement for specially trained maintenance personnel; 5. improve system availability by the effective selection and incorporation of Built In Test; Equipment (BITE); 6. allow removal and replacement of replaceable units without soldering and unsoldering.

3.11.5.1	The total, fully useable, addressable, physically present program instruction memory and data storage memory for each processor shall have at least 50% unused memory during the Normal Operations Mode over any 10 second period.
3.11.5.2	The processing speed of each processor shall be such that at least 50% of the throughput of each processor remains unused over all 10 second periods and at least 20% of the throughput of each processor remains unused over one second periods regardless of the system function performed.
3.11.5.3	The I/O channel reserve capability for each processor shall have at least a 50% reserve, addressable and useable, I/O channel capacity over any 10 second period.

3.13.1.1	The TCS shall have ergonomically designed operator controls and displays for the 5th percentile female to 95th percentile male operator.

3.13.1.11	In addition to displayed alert messages to the TCS operator, auditory alerts to include tones shall also be provided.
