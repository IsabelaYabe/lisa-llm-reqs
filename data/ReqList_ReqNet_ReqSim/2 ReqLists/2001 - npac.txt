10.1.1.1	NPAC SMS shall be available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week with the exception of scheduled downtime and unscheduled downtime within the time frame defined in R10-3 and R10-5.
10.1.2.1	NPAC SMS shall be 99.9 percent reliable. This applies to functionality and data integrity.
10.1.3.1	NPAC SMS shall have unscheduled downtime per year less than or equal to 9 hours.
10.1.4.1	NPAC SMS shall support a mean time to repair of less than or equal to 1 hour, for unscheduled downtime.
10.1.5.1	NPAC SMS shall have NPAC initiated, scheduled downtime of less than or equal to 24 hours per year.
10.1.6.1	NPAC initiated downtime as defined in R10-5 does not include downtime needed for software release updates initiated by or collectively agreed to by the Service Providers.
10.1.6.1.1	NPAC shall be capable of monitoring the status of all of its communication links.
10.1.6.2.1	NPAC shall be capable of detecting and reporting all communication link failures.
10.1.7.1	NPAC SMS shall be capable of detecting and correcting single bit errors during data transmission between hardware components (both internal and external).
10.1.8.1	NPAC SMS shall complete processing of all sending transactions at the time of system failure when the NPAC SMS resumes processing.
10.1.9.1.1	NPAC SMS shall support functional components with on board automatic self checking logic for immediate fault locating.
10.1.9.2.1	NPAC SMS shall support continuous hardware checking without any performance penalty or service degradation.
10.1.9.3.1	NPAC SMS shall support duplexing of all major hardware components for continuous operation in the event of a system hardware failure.
10.1.9.4.1	NPAC SMS shall support hardware fault tolerance that is transparent to the Service Providers.
10.1.10.1.1	NPAC SMS shall notify Service Providers of the system unavailability via both the NPAC SMS to Local SMS interface and the SOA to NPAC SMS interface if the system becomes unavailable for normal operations due to any reason, including both scheduled and unscheduled maintenance.
10.1.10.2.1	NPAC SMS shall notify Service Providers via their contact numbers if electronic communication is not possible.
10.1.10.3.1	NPAC SMS shall include the following information in the notification: •	The reason for the downtime •	When the down time will start •	When the down time will stop •	An NPAC contact number
10.1.11.1	NPAC SMS shall ensure the capability of receiving, processing and broadcasting updates will be given the highest priority during any maintenance, if resources allow only partial functionality.
10.1.12.1.1	NPAC SMS shall provide tolerance to communication link outages and offer alternate routing for such outages.
10.1.12.2.1	NPAC SMS shall offer alternate routing during communication link outages.
10.1.13.1.1	NPAC SMS shall, in cases where Service Providers have been switched to a backup or disaster recovery machine, adhere to a maximum time to repair of 4 hours for the primary machine.
10.1.13.2.1	NPAC SMS shall ensure that the time to switch the Service Providers to another machine and provide full functionality must not exceed the mean time to repair.
10.1.13.3.1	NPAC SMS shall restore the capability of receiving, processing and broadcasting updates within 24 hours in the event of a disaster that limits the ability of both the NPAC and NPAC SMS to function.
10.1.13.4.1	NPAC SMS shall restore full functionality within 48 hours, in the event of a disaster that limits both the NPAC and NPAC SMS ability to function.
10.1.14	NPAC shall provide reliability reports documenting the following: •	Schedule down time •	Unscheduled down time •	Mean time to repair •	System availability on a monthly basis to the Service Provider
10.2.16.1	NPAC SMS will have the capacity to support a user group in the NPAC sized for the region they service.
10.2.18.1	NPAC SMS shall ensure that the data storage of the History file must keep track of all transactions made for a tunable parameter period of time (default of one year).
10.2.19.1	NPAC SMS shall ensure that from the time an activation notice, modification or deletion request is received from a Service Provider until the time the broadcast of the update is started to all Service Provider local SMS will be less than 60 seconds.
10.2.20.1	NPAC SMS, under normal operating conditions, shall ensure that the response time from when a request or transaction is received in the system to the time an acknowledgment is returned will be less than 3 seconds for 95% of all transactions. This does not include the transmission time across the interface to the Service Providers’ SOA or Local SMS.
10.2.21.1	NPAC SMS shall be expandable to handle future growth due to circumstances described as follows: •	Added areas of portability •	Added Service Providers

10.3.3.1	NPAC SMS shall send an electronic notification to the Service Provider’s Local SMS indicating the time the NPAC will switch them back to the primary machine.
10.3.4.1	NPAC SMS shall sync up the database in its primary SMS with any updates sent to the backup or disaster recovery machine during the downtime.
